"Chuck Berry"

Father of Rock 'N' Roll

Berry during a live concert performance

Here's a time line of Chuck Berry life:

  • 1926 - Born in St. Louis, Missouri.
  • 1947 - Berry served three years in the Intermediate Reformatory for Young Men outside of Jefferson, Missouri, before gaining release on good behavior.
  • 1948 - Berry married Themetta "Toddy" Suggs, with whom he would eventually have four children.
  • 1951 - They played at local Black nightclubs in St. Louis, and Berry quickly developed a reputation for his lively showmanship.
  • 1952 - He met Jonnie Johnson, a local jazz pianist, and joined his band, the Sir John's Trio. Berry revitalized the band and introduced upbeat country numbers into the band's repertoire of jazz and pop music.
  • 1950 - Berry began taking road trips to Chicago, the Midwest capital of Black music, in search of a record contract.
  • 1955 - Wrote and recorded a song called "Maybellene" reaching No. 1 on the R&B charts and No. 5 on the pop charts.
  • 1956 - Berry quickly followed with a slew of other unique singles that continued to carve out the new genre of rock 'n' roll: "Roll Over, Beethoven," "Too Much Monkey Business" and "Brown-Eyed Handsome Man," among others.
  • 1985 - He received the Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award.
  • 1986 - He became the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame's first inductee.
  • 2017 - Berry dies at the age of 90. He is remembered as a founding father of rock 'n' roll, whose pioneering career influenced generations of musicians.
